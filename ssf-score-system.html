Quantified SSF evaluation model

SSF Score System

The SSF Score System turns the SSF doctrine into a repeatable 100-point evaluation model. It is not meant to replace written reasoning; it exists to make verdicts comparable, reviewable, and harder to hand-wave.

Secure: 40 pointsSimple: 30 pointsFast: 30 pointsDeveloped by: Obsidian

Scoring

Score model

Secure — 40

  • App isolation / sandbox: 10
  • System integrity: 10
  • Default network exposure: 5
  • Update model: 10
  • Privilege model: 5

Simple — 30

  • Single coherent system model: 10
  • User cognitive load: 10
  • Setup complexity: 5
  • Permission clarity: 5

Fast — 30

  • Idle resource usage: 10
  • Background services: 10
  • Responsiveness: 10
Verdicts

Verdict thresholds

ScoreVerdictMeaning
85–100SSF NativeThe system strongly embodies SSF within its declared scope.
70–84SSF Approved / ConditionalThe system is strongly aligned, but may require scope restrictions or have notable weaknesses.
50–69Borderline / Not approved under strict readingThe system has meaningful strengths, but fails one or more core expectations.
0–49Not SSF-approvedThe system does not satisfy the SSF baseline.
Control rule

Hard fail rules

A score alone cannot override a broken pillar.

  • If Secure < 20, the system cannot be SSF-approved.
  • If Simple < 15, the system cannot be fully SSF-approved for normal users.
  • If Fast < 15, the system cannot be fully SSF-approved for general daily use.
  • Scope can rescue a verdict only if the limitation is explicit and honest.
